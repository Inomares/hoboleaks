679541 During Pirate Insurgencies, pirate factions face off against the empires, each side attempting to 'score' systems by increasing the system's Corruption (pirates) or Suppression (militias) value through the completion of various activities and objectives. Each faction has an independent target score they are looking to reach to allow them to win the insurgency, with the minimum score required to win increasing for a faction as they win multiple events.

An insurgency starts with the placement of a pirate Forward Operating Base (FOB) in the warzone. Several systems are then selected adjacent to the FOB system as initial insurgency targets. This begins the ‘Forecasting Period'. The insurgency does not become immediately active, giving both pirate and militia capsuleers time to move ships into position and otherwise prepare themselves for the event.

Once the forecasting period ends, the main phase of the Insurgency begins. Insurgency activities begin spawning and both pirate and militia players are able to contest them. Each Insurgency activity is worth a number off Corruption or Suppression points. As pirates complete objectives they will increase the Corruption value of the system; as militia players complete insurgency activities they will increase the Suppression value of the system. A system reaching maximum Corruption scores a point for the pirates while a system reaching maximum Suppression scores a point for the empires.

When the Corruption value of a system hits stage 3 during an Insurgency event, the insurgency will spread - adding a new system to the growing insurgency zone - a new target for the pirates. Activities will begin spawning in this system immediately. This dynamic spread will prefer systems adjacent to the system which triggered the spread if possible and will not spread to systems with a security status of 0.8 and higher or 0.0 and lower.

When the pirates reach their target score they are deemed the winners of the insurgency. All systems with active insurgency will see their Corruption value increased to the next stage and the pirates will seize the FOB system as their own - removing it from the FW warzone and causing neighboring systems to become frontlines (see Factional Warfare guide for further information on frontline status). After some time, the pirates will then abandon this system, tenuously returning it to the previous owner with control at minimum and the infrastructure hub vulnerable.

If the empires reach their target score however, then the pirate FOB becomes vulnerable. The FOB has a single armor reinforcement timer and, when destroyed, will result in defeat for the pirates and victory for the empires. Following a victory by the empires, all systems in the insurgency will see their Suppression value raise by one level for some time before the event comes to a close.

There are additional rewards for players on both sides who participate in an insurgency. Participation rewards can be tracked from the insurgency window, with larger rewards paying out to players who participated on the winning faction.
